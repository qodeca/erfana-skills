# Code of conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the erfana community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

This project adopts the **[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)** as its code of conduct. The canonical, full text — including the detailed list of expected behaviors and of unacceptable behavior — is published at that link and is incorporated here by reference.

In short:

- **Do:** show empathy and kindness, respect differing opinions and experiences, give and accept constructive feedback gracefully, take responsibility for mistakes, and focus on what is best for the community.
- **Don't:** engage in harassment, demeaning or discriminatory conduct, personal or political attacks, publishing others' private information without consent, or any other behavior a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting. See the canonical text for specifics.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions) and when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported **confidentially** to **hi@qodeca.com**, monitored by the maintainer ([@marcinobel](https://github.com/marcinobel)). If you prefer, you can instead open a confidential report through GitHub.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer is obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

Maintainers will follow the **Enforcement Guidelines** of the Contributor Covenant 2.1 (correction, warning, temporary ban, permanent ban) when determining consequences for violations.
